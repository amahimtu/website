Vintage Band Merch

Memorabilia for the realest fans.


Overview.

The Product: Vintage Band Merch (VBM) is envisioned as the go-to e-commerce store for the resale of retro and vintage memorabilia. VBM appeals to your nostalgic longings by providing you with original vintage merchandise from huge bands like Matchbox Twenty to relatively low-key artists like Killing Time.

The Problem: Musicians and music enthusiasts the world over love band merch (I know I do), but merch is often seasonal, and—especially for more popular artists—can get sold out quickly and are high in value.

For instance, online, you can find old tour shirts from bands like Radiohead and Nirvana being sold for hundreds of dollars—sometimes for more than 10 times their original value—through websites like eBay and Etsy, meaning there is a market for cool vintage merch but no dedicated platform like VBM.

The Goal: Design the home page of a website dedicated to selling/reselling vintage band merchandise and memorabilia.

My Role: UI Designer designing the Vintage Band Merch website from conception to prototype.

Project Duration: February 2021

Note: This was a fun and challenging personal project I undertook to prototype the website for Vintage Band Merch, whereas the actual site does not exist yet. However, I do own the domain (vintagebandmerch.com). This particular project is more UI-/web design-focused with little to no emphasis on user research. I will walk you through my design process leading up to the prototype of the website.

Information Architecture: Sitemap.

I started by developing the sitemap for the website, taking inspiration from the information architecture of current existing music merchandise online stores. The sitemap is color-coded according to groups of activities that a user would interact with and experience.



2.
Ideation.


Paper Wireframe(s).

I then drew up some wireframes using just a notebook and pencil as shown below. The homepage consists of four sections: clickable hero image slider, a featured products section with carousel feature, 'about us,' and a site footer, which features a contact form, sitemap information, social media links, contact information, and copyright text.



UI Layout.

After drawing my wireframes, I utilized Figma to create a UI layout to shape the website. I decided to go for a 12-column layout—so that the site would be compatible with frameworks like Bootstrap—in a tiered layer cake style. The spaciousness and scannability provided by this kind of layout would lend the site a clean, contemporary feel. Even though most of the merch is vintage, I wanted to balance out the retro feel (via artifacts like the logo) with a modern design.



3.
Prototype.


Prototype.

Using the UI layout as a blueprint, I designed the mockup. I created the VBM logo—consisting of a vinyl record with the letters VBM—with Adobe Photoshop. For the hero images, I used artwork of Boards of Canada, Jimi Hendrix, and Steven Wilson, respectively.

After creating all the sections of the site, I prototyped the dropdown menus, hero image slider, and product carousel. Check out the prototype pathways outlining various combinations of user interactions.

Reflection & Takeaways.

Working on this case study not only allowed me to play with an idea I am passionate about, i.e., music merchandise and memorabilia, but also provided a great learning opportunity to practice my UI and web design skills as I created a prototype including overlays, hero images, carousels, and an interactive contact form.

A couple of next steps pertaining to this project include:

  • User research and testing.
  • Creating a responsive version of the design for other devices like mobile and tablet.
  • Designing more pages and features of the website; for example, checkout and billing, filters, and so on.

As a musician, this was such an enjoyable passion project to work on. I hope to make Vintage Band Merch a fully realized e-commerce business one day.

Thanks for checking out this project. I welcome feedback!
