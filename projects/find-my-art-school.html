Find My Art School.

The world needs artists & designers now more than ever. Find the right art school.


Overview.

Product: Find My Art School is an app that provides valuable resources and information to art school applicants, helping them find the right college and/or program. Since art and technology are no longer mutually exclusive, the app has a strong emphasis on STEAM (science, technology, engineering, art, and mathematics). Users can browse and learn about art-focused colleges and programs, read curated blog articles to keep up with the latest ongoings in the art school world, utilize portfolio resources, find and connect with mentors, and take courses via a partnership with Kadenze, a creative technology education platform.

Problem: Universities and colleges offer a plethora of art programs and courses for students, while conventional college finder/ranking sites do not provide enough information about specialized art programs and schools.

Goal: Design an app for prospective art school applicants to find out about the right program for them as well as leverage resources to strengthen their college applications and skillsets.

My Role: Sole UX designer designing the app from conception to prototype.

Project Duration: March 2021—May 2021

1.
Research.


Foundational User Experience Research

I conducted five interviews and created empathy maps to understand the users I designed for and their needs. A primary user group identified through research was students/young adults interested in applying to art school but unsure of programs and schools to which to apply.

Users cited the plethora of programs offered by art colleges and universities today but the lack of specific resources for students to get in-depth insights about art schools, and a sense of difficulty making informed decisions about applying to them.



2.
Strategy.


Personas.

Based on the foundational UX research, I developed three personas to represent different groups of users: RJ Lee, Bill Forrest, and Ece Akkan. While each of their journeys is unique, they have one thing in common: each of them wants to find the best art school and/or program for them—one that is the right fit.

Competitive Audit.

Having successfully entered the ‘ideate’ phase, I conducted a competitive audit to identify who Find My Art School’s competitors might be, what is working for them and what isn’t. I identified three competitors: Niche, Kadenze, & US News Best Colleges. None of these services, however, offer quite what Find My Art School does. Niche (niche.com) has a much broader target audience, which includes college applicants to all kinds of disciplines, not just art schools. Additionally, niche.com includes information on only American colleges, whereas Find My Art School has an international scope. Kadenze, like Find My Art School, emphasizes STEAM curricula but is a MOOC/creative education platform; US News Best Colleges focuses mainly on college rankings.



Both US News Best Colleges and niche.com club art schools into more general categories (for example, fine arts and contemporary art programs are often put into the same category), unlike Find My Art School, which helps users get a more detailed look at art colleges to find the right program, even the more "niche" (no pun intended) ones.



3.
Ideation.


Crazy Eights.

In order to quickly come up with a host of ideas for Find My Art School, I performed the “Crazy Eights” ideation exercise, producing eight ideas in eight minutes.



While some ideas were based on things I thought of earlier, some were spontaneously cooked up during the exercise; for instance, while I thought of integrating some kind of virtual reality experience in the app at an earlier stage, the idea to include a “learn” feature occurred to me during the exercise itself. In either case, I found it useful to actually draw out these ideas as I started to get a better sense of how I could begin to flesh out the app.

4.
Evaluation.


Usability Testing.

With a lo-fi prototype in hand, it was now time to dive into research and conduct a usability test. I wanted to see how real users experienced the app, albeit in low-fidelity form.

Key Parameters:

  • Study Type: Moderated Usability Study/Concept Test
  • Location: United States and India; remote
  • Participants: 5
  • Length: ~30 minutes

Revised Wireframes and User Flow After Concept Testing.

After conducting the usability tests and synthesizing research insights, I made three key changes to the wireframes and user flow of the app:



  1. User Onboarding Part 1: Onboarding Screens.

    I created four onboarding screens to show users what they can do on the app. The onboarding screens will appear before the sign-up process so users don’t have to ‘commit’ before beginning to use Find My Art School.

  2. User Onboarding Part 2: Onboarding Questions.

    I created three questions (one per screen) to learn about users, including their preferred location, degree choices, and their areas of interest. Subsequently, the app would tailor content based on user preferences.

  3. Search via Map.

    Users can search for colleges on a map, providing a fun, interactive way for users to engage with the app, as well as learn more about the schools they are interested in and their surrounding areas and neighborhoods.

5.
Prototype.


High-Fidelity Prototype.

Building on the screen mockups, I designed a high-fidelity prototype of the app, depicting the app’s main features and functionalities. In addition to the more standard interactions, the prototype features an interactive map to search for schools and a VR "mode" I created for users to check out college campuses in more detail.




Reflection & Takeaways.

Find My Art School has been both a challenging and rewarding project. Through practicing the design thinking methodology, I sharpened my UX design skills and learned how to empathize with users. I observed firsthand how well-planned user research and testing resulted in successful iterations and improved designs. Additionally, by creating features like an interactive map and integrating a VR-like experience into the app, I’ve been able to take my design skills to the next level.

Thanks for checking out this project. I welcome feedback!
