Innovation Hub Design System.

Building a design system for the Innovation Hub at the University of Toronto.


Overview.

The Innovation Hub is a consultancy at the University of Toronto that uses design thinking methodologies to research and solve problems for various departments at the university.

Product: Design system for the Innovation Hub.



Problem: The Innovation Hub is known for performing comprehensive research and delivering stunning visual assets to clients. However, the ever-growing organization lacked a design system, making it difficult for the communications team to reduce redundancy, find visual assets, and manage design at scale through reusable building blocks.

Goal: To create a design system for the Innovation Hub to reduce redundancy, manage design at scale, bolster communication workflow, and ensure consistent yet dynamic branding and design for current and future communications teams.

Client: The Innovation Hub, where I served as the Digital Communications Team Lead.

My Role: Product designer and project manager. I spearheaded the creation and implementation of the design system, from proposing the project plan to the director of the organization, to mapping and iteration.

Project Duration: December 2021—April 2022

1.
Research.


Environmental Scan and Project Proposal.

Based on an environmental scan of the organization’s existing design assets and after interviewing the Senior Project and Research Assistants about existing resources, I recognized the need for a design system.



The project proposal plan was approved by the Manager of Innovation Projects (and co-founder) at the Innovation Hub.


User Interviews.

I interviewed the communications team (four members) about their experiences designing visuals (such as icons, reports, social media banners, presentations, and website updates) for the Innovation Hub, focusing on problems they ran into while accessing resources, and how their design process could be streamlined. With these pain points in mind, I planned to map the design system so that it meet the needs of users.


2.
Mapping.


Mapping Relevant Components.

Based on the environmental scan and user interviews, it was time to map the design system so that it contains relevant information and resources for the communications team. I determined that the system should contain four pages, each with its own unique sets of visual assets and components. The pages are: philosophy, style guide, best practices, and secondary brands.


3.

Having mapped out the design system, I designed the first draft.

I. Philosophy.

The philosophy page highlights who the Innovation Hub is and what design means at the organization. We decided to frame the Innovation Hub philosophy through three lenses: Our Voice, Our Brand, and Our Context.

Reflection & Notes.

The Innovation Hub design system is meant to serve primarily as an internal tool for designers at the consultancy to design visual assets consistently and at scale, in accordance with the organization’s brand. It is a comprehensive and dynamic document on which we are continuing to work.

It has proved immensely useful to me and my design team so far as we have been working on projects and revamping websites and digital products. My goal is to ensure that it adds value to the current and future designers' workflow, and enhances the quality of the deliverables received by internal and external stakeholders.
